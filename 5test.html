The implementation of the LPG reforms in 1991 marked a turning point for India, ushering in a period of economic development. This growth, witnessed post-1991, not only contributed to a decline in poverty rates but also paved the way for the emergence and expansion of a bourgeois class.

India's cultural ethos has been significantly shaped by the evolving values of the urban middle class and bourgeoisie, notably exemplified by the rise in consumerism. The desire for material possessions, the adoption of global trends, and the influence of media and technology have all contributed to shaping the cultural ethos. This shift is often reflected in attitudes towards fashion, entertainment, and leisure activities.
