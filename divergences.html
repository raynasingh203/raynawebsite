The War On Local Languages: How Linguistic Hegemony Threatens Education In The Developing World

The Neglect of Local Languages

-The rush to introduce global or regional languages in education neglects local languages.

-Disadvantaged communities bear the brunt of the educational focus on global and regional languages.

-Despite advancements in technology and global connectivity, UNESCO reports that 53% of children in low- and middle-income countries struggle with reading comprehension by age 10

-When linguistic hegemony prevails, indigenous stories become niche markets, and connections with unique cultures are lost.

-Global languages dominating thus leads to the marginalization of vernacular languages, impacting cultural identity.
